Presenter Helper Notes — Official College YouTube Channel

Interactive prompts, audience questions, and talk-track support aligned to youtube-channel.html (13 slides).

Slide 01/13 — Title

Goal: Set context, establish credibility, invite attention.

Key Message

  • Platform = Knowledge + Branding + Revenue.
  • Student & faculty expertise already exists.

Audience Prompts

  • "How many of you already watch YouTube for learning?"
  • "What if our college becomes the source?"

Slide 02/13 — Problem & Opportunity (WHY)

Goal: Align on pain points and opportunity.

Key Message

  • Time spent on social media is high but underutilized.
  • Knowledge is repeated and not preserved digitally.
  • Opportunity: convert time + talent into value.

Audience Prompts

  • "Which topic gets repeated most in your classes?"
  • "How much of our best work is lost after graduation?"

Slide 03/13 — Proposed Solution (HOW)

Goal: Present the 3-pillar solution.

Key Message

  • Video knowledge database (reusable learning assets).
  • Brand visibility through official digital presence.
  • Revenue via ads to sustain the ecosystem.

Audience Prompts

  • "Which department should pilot first?"
  • "What content would you watch weekly?"

Slide 04/13 — Vision & Objectives

Goal: Show measurable objectives and intent.

Key Message

  • Position VIET as a digital knowledge hub.
  • Improve access, reduce repetition, build visibility.

Audience Prompts

  • "What outcome matters most: learning, branding, or revenue?"
  • "Which objective should be our first KPI?"

Slide 05/13 — Benefits to Stakeholders

Goal: Show value to students, faculty, college, and long-term.

Key Message

  • Students gain portfolio and resume weight.
  • Faculty save time with reusable content.
  • College builds brand + admissions reach.

Audience Prompts

  • "Would students add published content on resumes?"
  • "Which department benefits fastest?"

Slide 06/13 — Why This Will Work

Goal: Highlight manpower advantage + governance.

Key Message

  • Manpower is the differentiator.
  • Content pipeline already exists.
  • Institutional control ensures quality.

Audience Prompts

  • "Where can we recruit 10 content volunteers quickly?"
  • "Which committees should lead approvals?"

Slide 07/13 — Content Database Structure

Goal: Show organized structure and repeatable categories.

Key Message

  • Branch-wise library + standard content categories.
  • Balance regular content and special events.

Audience Prompts

  • "Which category should go first: lectures or labs?"
  • "What event is most watchable for the public?"

Slide 08/13 — Monetization & Analytics

Goal: Explain RPM model clearly.

Key Message

  • Revenue = (Views ÷ 1,000) × RPM.
  • Education/tech RPM range is ₹30–₹150.

Audience Prompts

  • "Which metric should we track weekly: views or watch time?"
  • "What is a realistic RPM target for us?"

Slide 09/13 — Revenue Analytics by Video Length

Goal: Compare content formats (Shorts, Standard, Long, Live).

Key Message

  • Shorts = reach, Standard = stability, Long = depth.
  • Live streams boost engagement and visibility.

Audience Prompts

  • "Which format should we prioritize in month one?"
  • "What campus event is ideal for a live stream?"

Slide 10/13 — Technical Setup

Goal: Show feasibility with minimal equipment.

Key Message

  • OBS Studio + smartphones are enough to start.
  • Student-led production reduces costs.

Audience Prompts

  • "Who already has editing skills?"
  • "Which lab can serve as a mini studio?"

Slide 11/13 — Governance & Quality Control

Goal: Build trust with approval framework and transparency.

Key Message

  • Faculty committee for approvals.
  • Monthly analytics + college-owned AdSense.

Audience Prompts

  • "Which faculty should be on the review panel?"
  • "How often should we report performance?"

Slide 12/13 — Implementation Roadmap

Goal: Align on phased execution.

Key Message

  • Phase 1: pilot (2–4 weeks).
  • Phase 2: scale (3–6 months).
  • Phase 3: expansion (6+ months).

Audience Prompts

  • "Which department is ready for the pilot?"
  • "What’s a realistic upload cadence?"

Slide 13/13 — Approval & Q&A

Goal: Close with clear asks and invite feedback.

Key Message

  • Approval for official channel + governance.
  • Basic infrastructure support.
  • QR feedback request.

Audience Prompts

  • "Any concerns about content quality or policy?"
  • "What additional support can we secure today?"
Tip Keep interactions short: 10–15 seconds per prompt. Invite 1–2 quick responses, then move forward.
